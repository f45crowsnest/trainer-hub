Engagement 3

The Golden Point

The Hard Truth

The hardest part of coaching isn't the workout.

It's getting them to come back tomorrow. And the day after. And the month after.

Anyone can sign someone up. Keeping them is where the real coaching happens.

Why They Leave

If all you do is count reps, scream loud, and tell people to push harder, they will quit. A couple of weeks. A couple of months at the latest.

Because workouts are everywhere. Apps. Other gyms. The free YouTube class their friend just sent them.

A boring class is an empty class. Doesn't matter how good your programme is. If they don't feel something during their time with you, they're gone.

The Golden Point

So you have to find the engagement point. I call it the Golden Point.

One thing. One specific thing about every member that you can come back to, every single time you see them.

Not "how was your weekend?". Anyone can ask that. Be specific.

  • The mum chasing her kids all day long: ask what activity her kids do, and try to connect.
  • The guy chasing a heavier deadlift: push him to the max every set.
  • The one who's getting married, having a baby, buying a house: write it down, bring it up next class.

One thing that's theirs. That's the Golden Point.

Be A Real Coach

A normal trainer counts reps and asks "how was your day?"

A real coach brings something. A point. A piece of content. A laugh. Something new, every single day.

Why It Matters

Members don't pay for the workout. The workout is everywhere. They pay for being seen.

Find the Golden Point with every member. Use it every time you see them.
