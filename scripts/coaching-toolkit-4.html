Coaching Toolkit 4

The Mission of a Coach

Hook 0:00 to 0:20

Today I want you to think about one thing.

What's the purpose of being a coach?

To push people? No.

To build muscle? Not quite.

Your job is to make people love training.

Because if they hate every session, your program does not matter. They will not come back.

Don't Be That Coach 0:20 to 1:25

Think about the worst coach you ever had.

The soccer coach who screamed at you for missing a goal.

The S and C coach who made you feel weak in front of the team.

The group class instructor who never looked at you once. The personal trainer who beat you up every session and never asked how you felt.

You never went back to that sport. You stopped showing up to that class. You cancelled that PT.

If that is happening in your gym, sit down and think.

Is it because the program was bad? Or is it because somewhere along the way, somebody made them feel like exercise was not for them?

You have that power right now. Every single session.

Don't be that coach.

Your mission is to make people love training.

Question One: How Do You Make Them Enjoy It? 1:25 to 2:00

Ask yourself a real question.

How do you make your client enjoy training with you?

Not the program. You.

The energy. The banter. The "I saw you nail that lift today." The walk-out at the end where they feel lighter, not crushed.

Read the room. Match the gear. Some days you push them. Most days you walk them through.

If they need a coffee just to recover from your session, you got the dose wrong.

Question Two: What's Your Specialty? 2:00 to 2:20

Question two. What is your specialty?

If you can do everything, you are a Swiss Army knife coach. Technically capable. Nobody's first call.

Pick one thing.

The cranky knees guy. The first strict pull-up coach. The lady who is scared of the weights corner.

Own it. So when someone in the room has that problem, your name is the only one that comes up.

Question Three: Do You Have A System? 2:20 to 2:40

Last one. Do you have a system?

A signature way you run things. The same warm-up cue. The same way you progress weights. The same way you finish a class.

So a client trained by you looks different from a client trained by anyone else.

That is what people remember. That is what they refer their friends for.

Close 2:40 to 2:55

Your job is not to push people.

Your job is to make them love training.

Once they love it, they show up. Once they show up, the results come for free.
